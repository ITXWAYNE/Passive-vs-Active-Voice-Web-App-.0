Active and Passive Voice

Lesson Objectives

  • Understand the difference between active and passive voice.
  • Convert sentences from active to passive and vice versa.
  • Know when to use the passive voice effectively.

1. What Is Voice in Grammar?

In grammar, voice shows whether the subject of a sentence is doing an action or receiving it. The two main voices in English are:

  • Active voice: The subject performs the action.
  • Passive voice: The subject receives the action.

2. Active Voice

Definition: The subject performs the action of the verb.

Structure: Subject + Verb + Object

  • The cat chased the mouse.
  • Maria writes a letter.
  • The teacher gave us homework.

3. Passive Voice

Definition: The subject receives the action. The agent (doer) can be added using by.

Structure: Subject + (form of "be") + Past Participle + (by + Agent)

  • The mouse was chased (by the cat).
  • A letter was written (by Maria).
  • Homework was given (by the teacher).

4. How to Convert Active to Passive Voice

  1. Identify the subject, verb, and object.
  2. Make the object of the active sentence the subject of the passive sentence.
  3. Use the correct form of be + past participle.
  4. Optionally include by + agent.
  5. Adjust tense accordingly.

Example:

Active: The students read the book.
Passive: The book was read (by the students).

For complex tenses:

Active: The committee is evaluating the proposal.
Passive: The proposal is being evaluated (by the committee).

5. When to Use Passive Voice

  • When the doer is unknown or unimportant.
  • When focusing on the action or the receiver.
  • In formal or scientific writing.
  • To sound polite or indirect (e.g., “Mistakes were made.”).

6. Active vs Passive Examples

Active Voice Passive Voice
The chef prepares the meal. The meal is prepared (by the chef).
They will announce the winner tomorrow. The winner will be announced tomorrow.
Someone has stolen my wallet. My wallet has been stolen.
The company is building a new factory. A new factory is being built (by the company).

7. Practice Task

Convert these active sentences into passive:

  1. The scientist discovered a new element.
  2. They will deliver the package by noon.
  3. The committee approved the proposal.
  4. Many people speak English worldwide.
  5. The mechanic is repairing the car.
